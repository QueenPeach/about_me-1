About Me:

I moved to Seattle 6 years ago from Omaha, NE after graduating from University of Nebraska-Lincoln. After moving to Seattle I started working as a Computer Engineer at a Fiber Optics company writing deviced drivers in C for a proprietary operating system and developer tools in Python.

In my spare time, I met my current wife, Natasha and we started riding bicycles, sailing, and generally taking advantage of all the outdoor activities that Seattle has to offer. During this time, I became obsessed with building road bikes using vintage steel frames acquired off various auction sites and modern components. I built four steel framed bicycles from the frame up including a 1974 Schwinn, a 1970s Chesini Arena, 1961 Legnano, and a 1970s Windsor Professional.

More recently, I developed another hobby of collecting, building, and tweaking mechanical keyboards. This is definitely a niche hobby although among the PC gaming community in South Korea it has become incredibly popular.

I'm generally interested in most tech and I really do like playing video games when I have the time. This was inspiration for me to acquire a raspberry pie and put the RetroPi image on it with some of my favorite vintage games. This turned out to be a great thing to play old games with friends on holidays.
